About Us

Club of Hearts - For The Children

We are a registered 501(c)3 non-profit organization dedicated to supporting the children of our community.

Our goal is to reach out to the youth in our community and assist them by educating, mentoring them, and simply being their for them. We do this by providing the services listed below:

  • Court Support
  • Education
  • Academic Intervention
  • Bully Pickups

Be assured we can support in other ways. If you have any questions on anything that is not listed below, please contact us and we will get back with you as soon as possible.

What We Do

Court Support

For a child that is living in fear due to abuse, we will go to court with them so that they aren't afraid to face the abuser and to tell the courts of wrong doings.

Education

We spread awareness of bullying and child abuse. We also help educate children and parents on the situations they are finding themselves in. We always attempt to provide the families with information and resources that can help.

Academic Intervention

With the schools permission our club goes to the school and sits down with disruptive children and shares our stories and paths we should have taken. Members of our group have successfully helped over 10 children change their outlook of school and authority.

After School Pickups

Children who are being bullied simply need friends and family. That is what a bully pickup is, it empowers the child being bullied by showing him or her and everyone else that the child has friends and family in his or her corner. These pickups are life changing for a child that is being bullied. If you know a child that can benefit from this service, contact us ASAP.

Know a child or family that needs support?

Contact us today! We are not here to intimidate. We are here for the children. Do not hesitate to contact us for any needs you may have. If we cannot provide the support you seek, we may have resources to help you find the support you need.

Frequently Asked Questions

These are the most common questions we receive. There is no such thing as a "dumb" question. So please if these do not answer your questions, contact us today!

  • Are you guys a motorcycle club?

    Yes and no. We are not your typical "MC". We love our motorcycles and we enjoy our rides, however, we focus more on being a non-profit organization that is here for the children.

  • What is an After School Pickup or a Bully Pickup?

    An after school pickup, or bully pickup, is where we as a group ride to the bully victims school (with the parent's and school's approval) and pick the victim up on our motorcycles and take them home or to a destination that the parent specifies. Nine times out of ten, the bullies will stop bullying the victim when they see that the victim has a lot more friends, especially "biker" friends. It also empowers the child by showing him or her that they have support and they are not alone.

  • Do you have to ride a motorcycle to join?

    No! You do not have to ride a motorcycle to join the Club of Hearts. However, you will not be able to participate in bully pickups or other events where motorcycles are the main focus. There are other events or missions as we call them where you may be needed, such as court support missions, education, and even academic intervention.

  • I don't have a lot of time, but still want to support. Can I join still?

    Yes you can! We have multiple types of members in our organization. We have the Knights, Full Members, and Support Members. Our full members are expected to participate in as many events as possible. Our support members are the members who support when they can but are not expected to be there all of the time. It is really up to you on which one you believe you are and which one you can commit to being.

  • Is the Club of Hearts a non-profit organization?

    Yes! We are a registered 501(c)3 non-profit organization dedicated to supporting the children of our community.

The Knights

Our club does not have the normal structure of a "motorcycle club". We focus more on being a non-profit organization. With that being said, our club has what we call, the round table. The individuals listed here are members of the round table, called the knights. They are the individuals who vote on anything and everything regarding the club. That is events we participate in, members who can join, changes within the club, anything related with the club is voted on by the knights.
